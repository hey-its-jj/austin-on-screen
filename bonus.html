This is another Richard Linklater joint. Everybody Wants Some!! follows the exploits of a college baseball team as it prepares for its first big playoff of the year—1980, that is—navigating the implications of masculinity, love, teamwork and purpose—all while crashing as many parties as possible, of course. This is without a doubt the spiritual successor to Dazed & Confused, which means the plot isn’t necessarily the focus. It’s hard to summarize well.

THE LOCATIONS

Okay, okay—so this one was shot in San Marcos, not Austin. It's still cool, though, because a good chunk of it was shot on the Texas State University campus in 2014. At first, I was a little bothered by it because I didn’t want to think about school while I was watching a movie. It’s funny; these are locations we all encounter every weekday. Seeing movie stars saunter past the Den or gather in front of Flowers Hall is weird.

If you're interested in seeing more, Then & Now Movie Locations has an excellent article on most of the filming locations in the movie.

IN THREE WORDS...

This movie is cute, meandering, and witty

I mentioned that this movie is the spiritual successor to Dazed & Confused. It’s very, very similar in a lot of ways. Like Dazed, Everybody Wants Some doesn’t rely on negative emotion. As a drama (dramedy?), it’s pretty tame; conflicts are set up in obvious, frankly cliché ways, but they never end in screaming matches or betrayals. Nothing shocking or traumatizing happens.

It's a refreshing approach if you ask me, but I can also see how it might not sit right with some viewers. Every time you suspect a plot thread is headed somewhere dramatic, you’re probably wrong. If you’re looking for melodrama, this is not your cup of tea. If you’re a little tired of that stuff, like I am, then this is a nice change of pace.

One more thing: I think this is a better movie than Dazed. Maybe it’s the advantage of having adult leads, or maybe it’s the wisdom of over 20 years, but the dialogue is so much wittier in this movie. The character interactions carry this movie, which I think is where Dazed fell flat. The leads can be really obnoxiously mean to each other, though. I didn’t like that so much. The sheer intensity of the hazing and razzing sacrifices a good deal of believability, not to mention charm. When the writing isn’t mean-spirited, though, it’s entertaining.
