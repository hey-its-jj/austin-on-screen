Overall, this project went pretty smoothly. Even parking wasn’t as big of a headache as I thought it would be, although that’s not saying much considering it’s Austin. It was a headache, but it wasn’t a migraine. But all of that frantic, paranoid planning paid off: We visited all the locations on the list in a relatively short time and without dying. Before filming, I had this idea that we’d inevitably get tackled by some tweaked-out, angry businessowner, or like a random extant cryptid.

Still, there are a few things I’d do differently if I were to do this over. First off: the camera. The “ancient camcorder” aesthetic isn’t as charming as I’d imagined it would be. It’s less of an aesthetic and more of a hideous impediment. You can hardly make out my face as I’m talking; it’s like I’m broadcasting from Olympus Mons. Initially, I opted for the camcorder because I didn’t want to worry about storage space. Using my phone would necessitate lugging my expensive laptop around so I could transfer the video files over and make room for more footage. I guess I could’ve used the cloud, but uploading giant, uncut video files over spotty 4G probably wouldn’t have gone that well. If I were to make a habit of content creation, then I’d absolutely invest in a functional video camera. I didn’t go out and find this camcorder specifically for this project, by the by; it’s just a camcorder we’ve had lying around for ten-odd years.

Then there’s the audio quality. I might as well have invested in a lapel mic or something. I doubt they’re very expensive. I ended up using a free voice recording app and holding my phone up to my mouth, and while it definitely helped with the background noise, the clarity is sub-par. While we’re on the subject—my line delivery. It’s awful. I’m not used to being on camera and talk way too fast. I know everyone says you’re supposed to hate your own voice when you hear it played back to you, but I’m convinced that my voice and cadence are objectively infuriating. I want to give myself a swirlie every time I see myself on screen. I will never clone myself because it would invariably end with a deathmatch a la Highlander. It would begin with a deathmatch, actually.

Finally, now that I know what a project like this demands of me, I can say that I should’ve covered more filming locations. It was a good idea, I think, to keep it condensed for the first try, but there are so many locations in Austin that I wish I could’ve visited. Part of the reason for the scant approach is that I didn’t want to inconvenience my brother. If it were only me, I probably would’ve gone for more, and over multiple days. Still, having a filming partner made things WAY easier, and frankly, it made us look less conspicuous. Sadly, being mixed, I kind of have to think about how I look while I’m ambling around doing literally nothing.
